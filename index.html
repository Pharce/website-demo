About Dr. Morgan Chen

Dr. Morgan Chen is an interventional radiologist and AI researcher with over 15 years of experience in medical imaging and minimally invasive procedures. With dual training in medicine and computer science, Dr. Chen bridges the gap between clinical practice and technological innovation.

After completing a fellowship in Interventional Radiology at Johns Hopkins Hospital and advanced research training at MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), Dr. Chen has dedicated their career to developing AI-powered solutions that enhance diagnostic accuracy, improve procedural outcomes, and advance personalized medicine.

Currently serving as the Director of AI Research at University Medical Center and as an Associate Professor of Radiology, Dr. Chen leads a multidisciplinary team focused on translating cutting-edge AI research into practical clinical applications.

Areas of Expertise

Interventional Oncology

Specialized in minimally invasive, image-guided procedures for cancer diagnosis and treatment, including thermal ablation, embolization, and targeted drug delivery systems.

Deep Learning in Medical Imaging

Development of convolutional neural networks and other deep learning architectures for medical image analysis, segmentation, and classification.

Computer-Aided Diagnosis

Creation of AI systems that assist radiologists in detecting and characterizing abnormalities in various imaging modalities, including CT, MRI, and ultrasound.

Procedural AI Assistance

Pioneering AI systems that provide real-time guidance during interventional procedures, enhancing precision and reducing complications.

Medical Image Processing

Advanced techniques for image enhancement, registration, and reconstruction to improve diagnostic quality and procedural planning.

Explainable AI in Healthcare

Research focused on making AI decision-making transparent and interpretable for clinical applications, addressing the "black box" problem in medical AI.
